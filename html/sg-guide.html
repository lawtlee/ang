Ang's SINGAPORE Flavor journey

food in singapore just hits different man

I called Singapore home for two whole months in the summer of 2023, and I spent every day eating my way across this vibrant island. This guide spills the beans on all the places I loved, from bustling hawker centers buzzing with energy to serene resturants that offer a quiet escape from the city's hustle. Singapore's mix of flavors is as vibrant as its multicultural streets, and I'm excited to lead you straight to the best bites. Get ready to fill up on everything from tangy Laksa to sweet kaya toasts that will tantalize your taste buds and leave you craving more.

üìçLei Garden Restaurant (Chijmes)

This Chinese Michelin star restaurant is located in Chijmes,a famous culturally important building in Singapore. It is also the place where the wedding scene in the movie Crazy Rich Asians was made! I went here with my family, but it was definitely on the pricier side. They have a set menu to order as well as a great selection of dim sum! Their appetizers are a must try :P

üìçEast Coast Food Village

My best of the best food experiences in Singapore are the hawker stalls. You really get to experience the local food culture and the authentic taste of the cuisine (for VERY CHEAP!). This food village is one of the largest and most popular ones, and I was fortunate enough to live very close to it.

I brought my mom here when she visited me. We got some really good chicken wings, fresh coconut water, and a roast fish for all under 30 dollars!

If there is one food that I constantly crave from Singapore, it is Kaya Toast. I had never had it before last year, but after getting it from Killiney, my life was forever changed.
