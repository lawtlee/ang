Ang's Japan culinary guide

places i went to in Japan that were just way too good

Last summer and again this spring, I wandered Japan's neon-lit streets, slurping ramen and savoring sushi right where it's best. This guide is my personal notebook, filled with the spots that made me say "wow" out loud. From cozy noodle joints tucked away in narrow lanes to bustling sushi bars where the fish is as fresh as the ocean breeze, here's where to eat in Tokyo and Kyoto to make your trip as delicious as it is unforgettable. Join me as I share my favorite discoveries, each one offering a unique slice of Japanese culinary culture.

üìçUSHIGORO S. Ginza

If you love yakiniku, this is probably one of the best places you can dine at. The layout of the restaurant is private rooms only, and you have to reserve your omakase course beforehand (make reservations EARLY!). Their different cuts of beef are super fresh, and a server will cook the meal right in front of you.

I have also never seen a restaurant so generous with their truffle shavings... (this is kind of a fancy dinner tho $$$$)

üìçMen-ya Inoichi
